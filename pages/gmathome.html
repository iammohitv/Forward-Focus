About GMAT

The Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT) is a computer adaptive test with a total duration of 3 hours and 30 minutes. The GMAT Exam Pattern comprises of both objective and subjective questions. The GMAT exam is a computer-adaptive test that assesses the candidates’ analytical writing, quantitative, verbal and reading skills in standard written English. This test is taken to get admission into a graduate management program, such as MBA and Masters in Finance related courses in top business schools across the world. GMAT exam is known to be the best predictor of success in management programs globally. Appearing for this exam and scoring well gives you an edge over the other students as it gives an idea of how motivated and committed you are to succeed in management career.

Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC) conducts GMAT every year to choose those candidates who will bring changes in the business world through analytical ideas. GMAC introduced a new section of Integrated Reasoning in GMAT in June 2012 to measure the data analytical ability of the test taker. Graduate school and Management school admissions committees assess your GMAT score, but along with that business schools will also get the list of interested candidates who prefer to study business.

What’s the takeaway? A high score on the GMAT will have a direct, positive impact on your graduate or Management school application.

What is tested on GMAT?

What’s tested on the GMAT? The GMAT measures your command of basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data analysis, as well as college-level vocabulary. More importantly, it measures your ability to analyze and evaluate written material, think critically, and solve problems.

Overall the GMAT test has 90 questions which are divided into four sections namely Analytical Writing Assessment, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning and Verbal Reasoning. Further, candidates are given a specific time to complete every section of the exam. A candidate has the option to take two ‘8-minute breaks’ during the exam. The test is formulated to examine analytical as well as logical thinking abilities of the test takers.

What is a good GMAT score?

A "decent" GMAT score is just in respect to the MBA of Masters projects to which you are applying! You should do some exploration on the schools to which you need to send your scores and set yourself practical objectives in light of the normal scores acknowledged by those projects. If you can find the mean or average GMAT score of admitted applicants, you’ll be able to determine what GMAT score will make you a competitive applicant.

Top level schools will generally be searching for a score in the 90th percentile or above (eg Wharton has a normal GMAT score of 713; Harvard 708; INSEAD 702; and London Business School 680).

The general GMAT score ranges from 200 to 800. The normal GMAT score among test-takers worldwide is 540. 66% of test takers score in the vicinity of 400 and 600.

The Analytical Writing Assessment is scored from 0 to 6 in half-point increments, and the mean score is 3.5-4. A score for the Integrated Reasoning segment, going from 1 to 8, with the mean score as 4.29.

The average quantitative GMAT score is 38.91. As you’ll see in the chart, you need top math scores to score above the 80th percentile.

How to send the scores to your Preferred Institutions?

The Score Report feature of the GMAT gives you the options to send the GMAT test scores from whichever test date you want schools to see. Note that even with the Score Reports option, you’ll want to perform your best on the GMAT the first time so you don’t have to retake the exam and pay an additional testing fee. You can view your scores by loging in to this GMAC website.

Where is the GMAT offered?

The computer-delivered GMAT is administered year-round on select test dates at testing centers. The GMAT™ Online exam is available in most locations, with the exception of: Mainland China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Sudan and Slovenia due to regulatory and local data privacy rules. Seating for GMAT is on a first-come, first-serve basis, and every year, around 2 lakh aspirants in India attempt the Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT Exam). It is obvious for the applicants spread across the nation to prefer finding test centres closest to their places. Since this exam can be attempted any time throughout the year, you can acoordingly choose the date and location as per your convenience. Nevertheless, while booking slot for your GMAT, you need to ensure that the date and time you choose is also available at the test centre of your choice. You can view a full list of testing centers, test dates, and seat availability on the given website"

It is highly recommended that you register for your preferred GMAT test date early so you can select a date that will allow enough time for GMAC to process your scores and send them to the Top Tier Management institutions you’ll be applying to. This will take within 20 days of the exam taken on the official website. You can take the computer-delivered GMAT once every 16 days, up to five times within any continuous rolling 12-month period (365 days).

You can register for the GMAT online via GMAC. For GMAT-related questions, you can find all the important information on GMAC here.

When to take the GMAT

You should take the GMAT any time that you have 2-3 months, or approximately 80-100 hours, to adequately prepare for it. Remember that your GMAT score is good for five years, so you can take the GMAT whenever you're ready – even if you don't plan on applying to business school for another year or two.

It’s a good idea to have a sense of when you’ll be applying to business school. Once you know when the application deadline is for the round you’ll be applying in, you can work from there.

How much does it cost to take the GMAT?

The cost to take the GRE is $250 for all testing locations. One needs to pay $50 for rescheduling exams on different dates. An amount of $80 is also charged if a candidate cancels an exam. The fee includes sending score reports to up to five graduate institutions of your choice.
